Gear

While only a camera is really all that one needs to get started taking amazing photos, there are some tools that I have found to be very useful.

Case:

Most camera's come with a case; however, investing in a good, quality case can really make a difference. It can allow room for expansion if you get more batteries, lenses, etc. It will keep your camera safe when traveling from location to location. No quality does not necessarily mean expensive. There are plenty of quality cases that are available at affordable prices. Doing a little bit of research will help to find the case that is right for you.

Tripod:

Having a solid tripod is something that I have used in almost every photo shoot that I do. Having that steady surface to put the camera so there is no shake (especially when taking photos in low light) really helps. Even when I am hiking quite a bit, I tend to want to bring at least a small portable tripod that I can set up on a rock or the dirt so I can get the pictures I want.

There are many different brands to choose from when buying tripods. I personally like Manfroto as they are very robust and last a long time, but they can be on the more expensive side. There are plenty of good quality tripods to be found at cheaper prices. One just might have to research a bit before picking one.

Polarizing Lens:

A polarizing lens is a very simple little filter that sits over the end of your lens. While it is small and usually pretty inexpensive, it helps to create some beautiful pictures by color correcting some of the light that the camera sees.

Normally the blue sky is a little difficult for the camera to see and so it will look more washed out in pictures. Putting a polarizing lens on helps to keep the sky nice and blue as well as keep other colors that might normally get lost. All of my lenses have one of these on them.

Different Lenses:

This is probably the most expensive out of all the gear you can get. Lenses are extremely useful and diverse but can cost a pretty penny.

Telephoto lenses help to zoom in to things really far away. Macro lenses help focus on very small things. Variable zoom lenses help to change to range of your focus.

The list goes on. While one can get away with one maybe two lenses it definitely is helpful to have some specialty lenses for different occasions.
